About

Noha Hazzazi, Ph.D. is an Assistant Professor in the Electrical Engineering and Computer Science Department at Howard University’s College of Engineering and Architecture. Broadly, her methodological research focuses on process optimization in healthcare systems, software reliability, software safety, security, and artificial intelligence.

Dr. Hazzazi has led a joint effort with Boston University under the auspices of the Public Interest Technology University Network (PIT-UN) to advocate public interest considerations in technology policy. She collaborated with Stanford University and Georgetown University toward creating the Public Interest Technology University Network case study platform – https://pitcases.org.

She is a member of the Golden Key International Honor Society and Phi Beta Delta honor society and is a recipient of a Six Sigma certificate during an internship at Raytheon. Dr. Hazzazi is also a member of the American Association of University Women (AAUW), a non-profit organization that uses education, advocacy, and research to advance equity for women and girls.

Dr. Hazzazi received her Ph.D. in Information Technology from George Mason University in Virginia. As a member of an underrepresented group, she is committed to increasing diversity in the computer sciences. She currently serves as the faculty adviser for the Upsilon Pi Epsilon honor society.
